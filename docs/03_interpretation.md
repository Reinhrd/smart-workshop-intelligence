# Interpretation

This document explains the business interpretation of the Smart Workshop Intelligence outputs.

## 1. KPI Interpretation

The project analyzes workshop service data to understand revenue structure, service reminder opportunities, cross-sell potential, demand forecast, and customer prioritization.

## 2. Revenue by Category

Oil & Filter is the largest service category and acts as the main entry point for recurring workshop visits. However, non-oil services contribute a significant share of total revenue, which means oil reminders can be used as a trigger for cross-sell offers.

## 3. Oil Reminder Alert Tier

The alert tier separates vehicles into Monitor, Warm Reminder, High Priority, and Critical. The actionable group is Warm Reminder and High Priority, because these vehicles are closer to the estimated service due condition.

## 4. Personalized Service Interval

The service interval differs across usage segments. Heavy Daily vehicles require more frequent oil service reminders, while Light Commuter vehicles can be handled with longer reminder cycles.

## 5. Market Basket Rules

Market basket analysis identifies service items that frequently appear together. These rules can support next-best-offer recommendations in WA reminders or service advisor scripts.

## 6. Weekly Demand Forecast

The 8-week forecast helps the workshop estimate future visits, prepare mechanics, manage booking slots, and plan inventory for oil, filters, and other recurring service items.

## 7. Business Recommendation

The recommended strategy is to use oil service reminders as an entry point for cross-sell, while controlling campaign timing using demand forecast and prioritizing customers based on urgency and value.
